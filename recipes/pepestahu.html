Pepes Tahu Recipes

Description

Pepes is one of the traditional food from Sundanese people or you can also called it pais. The word pepes is from the word "Mais" and "Pais", which mean a food that wrapped by leaf. Pepes present from the year 1970. Its origin from the plantation and mountain people which often cooking food with leaf as their methode. The process to ripen pepes is long which make it posible for the people who work in the dry-land farm or wet-rice field work while cooking.

Ingridients

  • 10 piece of White Tofu
  • 4-5 grain Roasted/fried candlenuts
  • 5-6 clove Large garlic bulb
  • 3-4 clove Large/medium-sized shallots
  • 2.5 tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp Granulated sugar

    • Pelengkap:

  • Banana leaves, as needed
  • Whole large/medium bird's eye chilies (I used 15) as needed
  • Bay leaves (I used: 15 leaves) as needed

Steps

  1. Prepare the banana leaves; wash and drain them, and if they are fresh, wilt them. Remove the stems from the red chilies, wash thoroughly, and drain. Do the same with the bay leaves—wash thoroughly and drain.
  2. Grind the candlenuts, garlic, and shallots; add salt and granulated sugar, grind again, and set aside.
  3. Mash the white tofu, mix in the spice paste until well combined, and taste to check the seasoning. Note: You can taste the mixture before steaming, as it is safe to eat—it contains no raw eggs or raw meat.
  4. Take a banana leaf, place one bay leaf on it, add 2 tablespoons of the tofu mixture, and shape it neatly. Place a whole bird's-eye chili on top. Fold or wrap the banana leaf (similar to wrapping nagasari).
  5. Arrange the pepes in a steamer and steam over medium heat for 30 minutes. Once fully cooked and the leaves appear done, turn off the heat. Remove the pepes and let cool or serve.
  6. Note 1: If you like, you can mix basil leaves into the pepes mixture for extra flavor. However, I didn't use them this time because my children aren't too fond of them.
  7. Note 2: If desired, the pepes can be grilled before serving—either in a non-stick pan or directly over a flame or charcoal.
  8. Note 3: The steaming time depends on the size and quantity of the *pepes* parcels; I steamed mine for 30 minutes because I had a large batch and used a rather small steamer that was packed full.
