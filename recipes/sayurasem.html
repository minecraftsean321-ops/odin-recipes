Sayur Asem Recipes

Description

So this is an Indonesian food that easy to make and suitable for boarder student to save their money. This food is known since 18th century when the dutch still colonized Indonesian. The recipes originally come from a simple, fiber-rich dish for the common people.

The combination of a refreshing tamarind-based broth and ingredients such as young jackfruit, sweet corn, *melinjo*, and long beans makes *sayur asem* a symbol of diversity and simplicity. Over time, this dish spread to various regions, giving rise to distinctive variations such as the Betawi and Sundanese versions.

Ingridients

  • 2 cloves Red onion
  • 1 cloves of Garlic
  • 4 pieces Red chili peppers (you can add more if you prefer it spicier)
  • 1 pieces Grilled shrimp paste

    • Stuffing:

  • Boiled Corn
  • Melinjo
  • Peanuts
  • Long beans
  • Tamarind dissolved in water as needed
  • Tamarind vegetable seasoning to taste (mine: 2 tbsp)
  • Water
    •

Steps

  1. Grind the shallots, garlic, red chilies, candlenuts, and roasted shrimp paste into a smooth paste.
  2. Saute all ingredients until fragrant and cooked.
  3. Pour in the water (I start with 500ml; more can be added if needed) and add all the vegetables.
  4. Adjust the seasoning by adding broth, salt, sugar, and tamarind water.
  5. Cook until everything is done.
