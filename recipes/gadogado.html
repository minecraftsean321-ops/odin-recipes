Gado-Gado Recipes

Description

Gado-Gado is a food that made up of vegetables, potato, tempeh, tofu, boiled eggs, which given a peanut sauce The origins of Gado-Gado is from the 17th century (1628-1629) when the army of Mataram Empire which led by Sultan Agung carried out raid on Batavia and then they ran out of food esecially rice. So that the armiy from warok Ponorogo affiliated with the war troops start to make peanut-based pecel sauce, then poured it to raw vegetables found aroung the rice fields to survive. In Javanese, this practice is called gadho, which means eating only the side dish—or eating the side dish without rice.

Ingridients

  • 1 bunch Spinach
  • 100 g Bean sprouts
  • 2 blocks of Tofu
  • 2 Cucumbers

Bumbu:

  • 50 g Peanuts
  • 7 Bird's eye chilies
  • 1 White garlic
  • A little Palm sugar
  • A little Tamarind water
  • Salt
  • Hot Water
  • Fried shallots (for sprinkling)

Steps:

  1. Wash the ingredients thoroughly. Blanch the water spinach and bean sprouts. Set aside.
  2. Fry the tofu
  3. Fry the bean, chilli and white garlic
  4. Blend all the fried spice ingredients, adding salt, tamarind water, palm sugar, and hot water. Taste and adjust the seasoning.
  5. Arrange the vegetables, cucumber, and tofu, then pour the sauce over them. Sprinkle with fried shallots.

Tips

It tastes better if you add lime.
